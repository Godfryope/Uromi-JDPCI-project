COMMEMORATION OF THE FEAST DAY OF ST. JOSEPHINE BAKHITA

Justice Development and Peace/ Caritas Initiatives (Uromi JDPCI) remarkably commemorated the feast day of St Bakhita also known as the ‘International Day of Prayers and Awareness against Human Trafficking on the 8th day of February, 2022 at the Catholic Chancery Complex, Egbele, Uromi 22 FEB 2022

The event which was attended by dignitaries from various parts of Edo State was also used to empower a total of 149 persons from different communities across the five LGAs in Edo Central Senatorial District. 75 of the beneficiaries were students selected from 26 Government Schools across the five LGAs in Edo Central Senatorial District through a structured assessment. The other beneficiaries were 41 caregivers from two different projects (Don't Pay With Your Life and SCOPPH-T) who were supported with grants for income generating activities while 33 young persons were empowered with start-up equipment and materials across 7 skills set comprising Hairdressing, barbing, Tailoring, Automobile, Machine Mechanic, Phone repair and Baking) by the SMAT-P project.

In the opening remark, the Executive Director, Very Rev Fr Dr Fidelis Arhedo emphasized the importance of the commemoration of the feast of St. Bakhita to Uromi JDPCI as one of the major actors in the fight against human trafficking. This is because St. Bakhita represents a symbol of hope for trafficked victims and victory over slavery.

The NAPTIP Zonal Commander, Benin Zonal Command; Barr Mrs Chidiebere Oruruo Ifufu also used the occasion to sensitize the participants on the dangers inherent in human trafficking while appreciating the laudable efforts and interventions of Uromi JDPCI.

The representatives of the Honourable Commissioner for Education, the AG Chairmen of Esan North East and Esan Central LGAs, and the Chief Inspector of Education; Esan North East while presenting their goodwill messages encouraged participants made up of Principals, students and caregivers to shun all forms of human trafficking adding that children should be supported and encouraged to go to school or a learn a vocation for a better future.
