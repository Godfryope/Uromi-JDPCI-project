HUMAN RIGHT EDUCATION AND PARALEGAL SCHEME

22 FEB 2022

Uromi JDPCI in sustaining her commitment to build the capacity of the various Community Human Right Clubs across 30 communities of the project scope on how they can effectively protect and promote the human rights and dignity of members of their communities organized a refresher training on the “Overview of the Nigerian Legal System and Conflict Management” for Paralegals and Advocates who constitute membership of the 30 clubs.The training which was highly interactive was delivered by Theresa Odion Esq. and Mr. Michael Iyoriobhe respectively. Records of registered participants showed 101 (52 males, 49 females) inclusive of the 5 Seasoned Legal Practitioners and Supervisors of the 5 Local Government Areas of Edo Central Senatorial District. During the course of the training, participants were exposed to the Administration of the Nigerian Justice System in relation to arrest, search seizure, bail and bail processes amongst others.

They were also enlightened on the meaning and types of conflict, its stages, as well the different ways of resolving conflict. Emphasis was laid on litigations and Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) as some of the ways of resolving conflict. The benefits of employing ADR in resolving conflicts was extensively treated, while stressing that ADR can only be used in Civil matters alone and not for criminal matters such as rape and defilement.

Uromi JDPCI is optimistic that this refresher training would help enhance productivity of the Human Right Paralegal/ Advocates in their communities.

The commission is encouraging parishioners across the Diocese to continually perform acts of charity by reporting human rights violations to the human right advocates in the interest of Justice and peaceful co- existence.
